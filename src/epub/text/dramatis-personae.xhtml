Dramatis Personae

  • The Bezúkhovs

    • Count Kiríl Vladímirovich Bezúkhov, a wealthy old grandee of the Empress Catherine’s time. At his death his illegimate son Pierre inherits his title and estates.

    • Monsieur Pierre (20; all ages as of 1805), afterwards Count Pyotr Kirílych Bezúkhov. The Count’s illegitimate son, educated abroad, and easily led both into dissipation and into idealistic theories of life.

  • The Bolkónskis

    • Prince Nikoláy Andréevich Bolkónski. A harsh martinet, full of old-time prejudices, living a bitter, lonely life at his estate of Bald Hills; father of Prince Andréy and Princess Márya.

    • Prince Andréy Nikoláevich Bolkónski (27); Andrúsha, André. Adjutant to General Kutúzov; wounded at Austerlitz; proprietor of Boguchárovo.

    • Princess Lizavéta Bolkónskaya (née Mienen); Lise, Liza, “little princess.” Prince Andréy’s wife.

    • Prince Nikoláy Andréevich Bolkónski; Nikolúshka, Koko, Nikólenka. Prince Andréy Nikoláevich’s baby son.

    • Princess Márya Nikoláevna Bolkónskaya; Másha, Máshka, Marie. Marries Nikoláy Ilyítch and becomes the Countess Rostóva.

  • The Drubetskóys

    • Prince Borís Drubetskóy; Borénka. A relation of the Rostóvs; he is cold, calculating, and selfish, and through influence is rapidly advanced.

    • Julie Drubetskáya (21), née Karágina. Marries Borís’.

    • Princess Anna Mikháylovna Drubetskáya. Poor but intriguing; Borís’ mother.

  • The Kurágins

    • Prince Vasíli Sergéevich Kurágin; Basile. Close relative of Count Kiríl Bezúkhov.

    • Prince Ippolit Kurágin; Hippolyte. Prince Vasíli Sergéevich’s older son; in the diplomatic service, but dissipated and foolish.

    • Prince Anatole Kurágin. Prince Vasíli Sergéevich’s younger son; a spendthrift who aspires to Princess Márya Nikoláevna’s hand, but proves himself unworthy.

    • Princess Elèna Vasílievna Kurágina; Elèn, Hélène. Prince Vasíli’s daughter, rumored to have had an affair with her brother Anatole.

  • The Rostóvs

    • Count Ilyá Andréevich Rostóv. A wealthy but extravagant proprietor whose affairs go from bad to worse.

    • Countess Natálya Rostóva (née Shinshiná), his wife.

    • Count Nikoláy Ilyítch Rostóv (20); Nikolúshka, Nikólenka, Nicolas. Count Ilyá Andréevich’s eldest son, open-hearted, gallant, generous; serving in the cavalry.

    • Count Pyotr Ilýnich Rostóv (9); Pétya. Ilyá Andréevich’s youngest son.

    • Countess Véra Rostóva (20). Count Ilyá Andréevich’s oldest daughter; later married to Alphonse Berg.

    • Countess Natálya Ilyníchna Rostóva (13); also called Natásha, Natáli, Nathalie. Count Ilyá Andréevich’s youngest daughter.

    • Sófya Alexándrovna (15); also called Sónya, Sonyúshka. Count Ilyá Andréevich’s orphan niece.

  • Others

    • Márya Dmítrievna Akhrosímova. Respected and feared for her common sense and frankness, known as le terrible dragon.

    • Osip Alexéevich Bazdéev. The Freemason, Pierre’s “Benefactor.”

    • Alphonse Kárlovich Berg. A conceited young officer who “gets on” and marries Countess Véra Rostóva.

    • Vasíli Dmítrich Denísov. Also called Váska, Vasíli Fëdorovich; gallant soldier and poet, in love with Natásha Rostóva.

    • Fédya Dólokhov (25). A gambler and roué, who offers himself to Sónya but is rejected; brave but bad.

    • Márya Ivánovna Dólokhova. The mother of Fédya Dólokhov.

    • Mikháil Ivánovich. Prince Nikoláy Bolkónski’s live-in architect.

    • General Mikháil Ilariónovich Kutúzov. Real-life commander-in-chief of the Russian army during Napoleon’s invasion.

    • Anna Pávlovna Schérer (40). Maid-of-honour to and favorite of the dowager empress Márya Fëderovna.
